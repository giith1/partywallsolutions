What is  the Party Wall Act

The Party Wall etc. Act 1996 provides a legal framework for preventing and resolving disputes related to construction work on party walls, boundary walls, and excavations near adjacent properties.

Under the Act, a Building Owner planning to carry out such work must formally notify the ‘Adjoining Owners’ in accordance with the prescribed procedure.

Failure to issue the required notice may lead to legal action, including an injunction to stop the construction.

What does the Party Wall Act do?

"The Party Wall Act grants ‘building owners’ the right to carry out construction projects, provided they adhere to specific conditions and legal procedures."

"It establishes a framework for preventing and resolving disputes, enabling the ‘building owner’ to proceed with the work even if the ‘adjoining owner’ objects."

"The Act also empowers ‘adjoining owners’ to challenge proposed works, ensuring they are executed correctly, following proper design sequences and specifications."

"Additionally, it offers legal protection to both parties in the event of damages arising from the construction."

Sections

Section 1 - New Walls

A new wall has been constructed up to the junction line to facilitate the development of a new rear extension.

Section 2 - Beam Installation

New steel beams have been installed to support expansions, open-plan layouts, or loft conversions.

Excavations - Section 6-1

Excavation carried out within 3 meters of a neighboring property and below the level of its foundations.

Excavations - Section 6-2

Excavation conducted within 6 meters of an adjacent property, where any part of the excavation intersects a 45° plane extending from the base of the neighbor’s foundations.
