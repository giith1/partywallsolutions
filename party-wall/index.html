Frequently Asked Questions

Our Most FAQ's On Everything Party Wall!

What is a party wall, and why is it important in neighbor relations?

A party wall is a shared wall that separates buildings or properties owned by different parties, typically found in semi-detached houses or terraces. It plays a critical role in neighbor relations because changes to or work on the party wall can impact both parties. Clear communication and legal compliance through a party wall agreement can prevent disputes.

What should I do if my neighbor plans to undertake work affecting the party wall?

If your neighbor intends to do work that affects the party wall, such as renovations or extensions, they are required to serve a Party Wall Notice under the Party Wall Act (in jurisdictions like England and Wales). You can either consent to the notice, request modifications, or dissent, which may lead to appointing a surveyor to manage the agreement.

What happens if there is a disagreement about the party wall work?

Disagreements are typically resolved through the appointment of a party wall surveyor. If both parties agree, a single surveyor can act for both. Alternatively, each party can appoint their own surveyor, who will then select a third surveyor to make impartial decisions if necessary. The surveyor's decision is binding.

Can I carry out work on a party wall without informing my neighbor?

No, you are legally required to notify your neighbor before carrying out work on a party wall. Failing to do so can lead to disputes, legal complications, and even an injunction to halt the work. Always comply with local regulations and serve the necessary notices to avoid issues.

How can I ensure smooth neighbor relations during party wall negotiations?

Maintaining clear and open communication with your neighbor is key. Inform them about your plans early, explain the scope of the work, and address their concerns. Hiring an experienced party wall surveyor and adhering to legal requirements also demonstrates your commitment to a fair process, which helps build trust and goodwill.
